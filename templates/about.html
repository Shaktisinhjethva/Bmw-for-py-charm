BMW Guidelines

Biomedical Waste Management Rules 2016 — Key requirements for hospitals and healthcare facilities in India.

📋 Legal Framework

The Biomedical Waste Management Rules 2016, notified under the Environment (Protection) Act 1986, mandate proper segregation, storage, transportation, and treatment of biomedical waste generated by healthcare facilities.

  • Every occupier (hospital) must treat waste on-site or through authorized Common Bio-medical Waste Treatment Facility (CBWTF)
  • Annual report submission to State Pollution Control Board (SPCB)
  • Maintain records of waste generated, handed over, and treated for minimum 5 years

🚚 Transportation Requirements

  • Dedicated vehicles with "Biohazard" label — no mixing with other waste
  • GPS-enabled tracking for real-time monitoring
  • Valid authorization from CPCB/SPCB for transporters
  • Manifest system: Form 12 for waste handover documentation
  • PPE mandatory for all handlers during collection and transport

⚠ Storage & Handling

  • Storage not exceeding 48 hours in temperate climate (24 hours in warmer regions)
  • Designated storage area away from patient zones
  • Leak-proof, puncture-resistant containers with biohazard symbol
  • Regular disinfection of storage areas
  • Needle cutters mandatory for sharps before disposal
